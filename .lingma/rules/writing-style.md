# Writing Style Guide

## Narrative Voice

### Perspective
- **First person**: Use "I/me/my" consistently throughout travel narratives
- **Dual gaze technique**: Maintain both past experience and present reflection voices

### Tense Usage
- **Past tense**: For travel narratives and events ("I arrived", "I walked")
- **Present tense**: For current reflections and commentary ("I realize now", "This reveals")
- **Mixed tense**: When past narrative is interrupted by present analysis

### Tone
- **Primary**: Reflective and introspective
- **Secondary**: Self-mocking and honest
- **Register**: Semi-formal to informal (personal narrative style)
- **Avoid**: Overly academic or detached language

## Language Level

### English
- **Vocabulary**: TEM-4 level (intermediate-advanced)
- **Grammar**: Standard English with occasional intentional fragments for effect
- **Expressions**: Natural, avoiding Chinglish patterns

### Chinese
- **Usage**: Notes, explanations, and meta-commentary only
- **Style**: Clear and concise
- **Function**: Provide context or supplementary information

## Content Structure

### Chronological Integrity
- Events follow strict chronological order within each journey
- Seven journeys maintain sequential numbering (Tour 1-7)
- Prelude episodes (1-2) come before main chronicles

### Narrative Arc Pattern
Each journey follows: **Ambition (Planning) → Action (Experiences) → Failure (Emptiness)**

### Dual Gaze Implementation
Example:
> Past "I": "I had meticulously planned every detail of the route..."
> Present "I": "I thought control meant respect. Now I see it was just an excuse to avoid real intimacy."

## Emotional Tone

### Primary Hue: Slate Blue
- Cold, introspective, like deep-sea diving
- Used for narrative descriptions and scene-setting
- Creates emotional distance and objectivity

### Secondary Hue: Rust Red  
- Physical shame of memories
- Used for moments of self-confrontation
- Visceral and uncomfortable

### Style Guidelines
- **Precise**: Like photography - specific details matter
- **Vulnerable**: Open about failures and shortcomings
- **Unapologetic**: Honest without excessive self-justification
- **Restrained**: Avoid melodrama or overwrought emotions

## Character Archetype

### "The Precise Waste"
Maintain these contradictions:
- **Over-planning**: Meticulous routes vs. emotional unpreparedness
- **Taking vs. Giving**: Craves connection but only knows how to consume experiences
- **Blindness in Present**: Always living for "next" moment, missing what's valuable now

## Sensory Details

### Preserve Vivid Descriptions
- **Heat**: "wall of heat", "humid air"
- **Sounds**: "tuk-tuks blared", "buzzed with laughter"
- **Smells**: Street food, incense, tropical air
- **Visual**: Light, shadows, colors, movement

### Cultural Context
- Maintain authentic Thai/Malaysian location names
- Preserve local terms (BTS, tuk-tuk, songkran, etc.)
- Keep IATA airport codes and flight numbers accurate

## Common Issues to Avoid

### Grammar & Style
- ❌ Chinglish expressions (direct translation from Chinese)
- ❌ Inconsistent tense within same narrative section
- ❌ Overuse of passive voice
- ❌ Excessive adjectives or adverbs
- ❌ Run-on sentences without proper connectors

### Narrative Problems
- ❌ Losing the dual-gaze balance (too much past OR present)
- ❌ Sentimentalizing or romanticizing experiences
- ❌ Explaining emotions instead of showing through actions
- ❌ Breaking chronological flow without clear reason
- ❌ Inconsistent characterization across chapters

## Editing Principles

### Minimal Changes
- Apply smallest edits necessary to fix issues
- Preserve author's unique voice and expression
- When in doubt, ask before making significant changes

### Clarification Needed When
- Emotional tone shifts dramatically without setup
- Timeline becomes unclear or contradictory
- Character motivation seems inconsistent
- Cultural details appear inaccurate

## Output Format for Suggestions

When providing editing suggestions:
1. **Issue Analysis**: Identify specific problems with examples
2. **Narrative Impact**: Explain how it affects the story
3. **Minimal Fix**: Show the smallest change that resolves the issue
4. **Alternative Options**: Provide 2-3 approaches if multiple valid solutions exist
5. **Voice Check**: Confirm the revision maintains dual-gaze technique
