Traveling as part of a tour group, we were offered an optional excursion to sites along the 38th Parallel for an extra fee. My fellow traveler, Wenwen, and I opted out. Consequently, we were left behind at a rest stop while the rest of the group continued their tour. Initially feeling somewhat isolated, we wandered into a nearby convenience store for coffee. There, we met the owner’s child, whose endearing nature and eagerness to play instantly bridged the language barrier. What began as a moment of abandonment turned into a delightful encounter, reminding us that human connection transcends spoken words.

## Weizhou Island

I have visited Weizhou Island twice, with my first trip taking place in July 2011.

My friend and I traveled by train from Chongqing to Nanning, transferred to another train for Beihai, and finally boarded the Feiyu fast ferry to the island.

Our initial accommodation was a place called "Piglet Bar," which proved to be neither comfortable nor budget-friendly. On our first night, however, we splurged several hundred yuan on a seafood feast, indulging in abalone, mantis shrimp, grouper, and, naturally, plenty of beer.

Seeking better value, we later moved to a local guesthouse run by the village head. The rates were far more reasonable, and the fees for processing our fresh catches were fair. We continued our culinary exploration, particularly savoring abalone served with rich sauce over steamed rice—a dish so delicious it could tempt one to eat several bowls. Not every experiment was a success, though; we tried shark meat, which turned out to be truly unpleasant. Its gritty texture, reminiscent of sand, made it quite unpalatable.
